WHAT ARE GRAVITATIONAL WAVES?

Gravitational waves are ripples in the
curvature of spacetime that are seen as waves at
the speed of light!

Einstein actually theorized
gravitational waves back in1916 by his
Theory of Relativity!

SO, HOW DOES IT WORK?

The more massive something is,
the more it curves spacetime!

Gravitational Waves - A little More Detailed

The strongest gravitational waves are

produced by catastrophic events

such as colliding black holes, the

collapse of stellar cores (supernovae), or

coalescing neutron stars or white dwarf stars
